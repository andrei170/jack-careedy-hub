D) Bottleneck Analysis

5 specific gaps identified in your current marketing infrastructure.

Gap 1

No Structured Info Product or Course

What is missing: Jack has a newsletter and a "3 Day Pitch Pack" but no structured course, coaching program, or high-ticket offer for filmmakers. He teaches for free via his newsletter but has no monetization system beyond the pitch pack and agency services.

Why it matters: He is sitting on a 1,000+ person email list of filmmakers who want to learn from him. Every week he gives away valuable content with no conversion mechanism to a paid product.

The fix: Build a structured offer: a $997-$2,997 course or $3K-$5K coaching program teaching filmmakers his system for landing high-ticket clients. The newsletter becomes the top of a funnel that feeds into the paid offer.

Gap 2

No VSL or Sales Funnel

What is missing: There is no video sales letter, no application funnel, no booking page for a strategy call. The newsletter opt-in is the only conversion mechanism visible.

Why it matters: Without a sales funnel, Jack is leaving money on the table every week. His 1,000+ subscribers see his newsletter and either buy the pitch pack or do nothing. There is no middle step.

The fix: Build a VSL funnel: newsletter opt-in feeds into a VSL page that pitches the coaching/course offer, which drives to an application form or booking page.

Gap 3

No Email Nurture Beyond Newsletter

What is missing: The newsletter goes out weekly but there appears to be no automated welcome sequence, no nurture sequence for new subscribers, no sales sequence for the pitch pack or any future offer.

Why it matters: New subscribers are warmest in the first 7 days. Without an automated welcome/nurture sequence, Jack is missing the highest-intent window to convert them.

The fix: Build a 7-10 email welcome sequence that delivers value, builds trust, shares case studies, and drives to the offer.

Gap 4

No Social Proof System

What is missing: No visible testimonials, case studies, client results, or student transformations on his website, newsletter, or IG. The 52 Stories podcast is the only third-party credibility marker.

Why it matters: Filmmakers considering paying for coaching need proof it works. "1,000+ filmmakers" on the newsletter is social proof, but it needs to be paired with transformation stories.

The fix: Collect testimonials from pitch pack buyers, agency clients, and newsletter subscribers. Display them on the landing page and in email sequences.

Gap 5

Small but Engaged Audience with No Paid Traffic

What is missing: 14K IG followers is decent but not massive. No evidence of paid ads, retargeting, or structured traffic strategy.

Why it matters: Organic alone caps growth. With strong cinematic content, Jack's video ads would perform extremely well on Meta. Filmmakers make the best ad content because they ARE the content.

The fix: Run Meta ads using his best-performing cinematic content. Retarget newsletter subscribers and IG engagers. Even $20-50/day would significantly accelerate growth.
