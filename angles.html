Campaign Angles

5 positioning angles with headlines and hooks, written in your voice for your filmmaker audience.

Angle 1: The Undercharging Problem

The reason you are undercharging is not your skill level. It is your positioning. Most filmmakers compete on price because they do not know how to compete on value. Jack Careedy teaches 1,000+ filmmakers how to land high-ticket clients by changing how they pitch, not what they shoot. The camera is the same. The price tag is not.

Angle 2: The Pitch Changes Everything
The Difference Between a $500 Gig and a $10K Project Is Not the Work. It Is the Pitch.

Two filmmakers walk into the same meeting. Same gear. Same reel. One walks out with a $500 job. The other walks out with a $10K retainer. The difference is the pitch. How you frame your value determines what you get paid. Jack Careedy built a framework called the 3 Day Pitch Pack that teaches filmmakers how to position, present, and close premium deals.

Stop Trading Hours for Money. Start Building a Creative Business.

Freelancing is a trap. You finish one project, and you are back to zero, hunting for the next one. There is no pipeline. No recurring revenue. No leverage. Jack Careedy went from shooting everything for anyone to building PhotoBomb Media and a community of 1,000+ filmmakers who are learning how to turn their cameras into real businesses, not just side hustles.

Angle 4: The 17-Year-Old Who Figured It Out
He Started Shooting Professionally at 17. By His Early 20s, He Had Built a Studio and a 1,000-Person Community.

Jack Careedy did not wait for permission. At 17, he was traveling 20+ countries as a professional photographer. Now he runs PhotoBomb Media and sends a weekly newsletter to over 1,000 filmmakers teaching them exactly how he did it. No degree. No agency internship. Just the work, the positioning, and a system for landing clients who pay premium rates.

Angle 5: Your Reel Is Not the Problem
You Keep Updating Your Portfolio. The Problem Is Not Your Work.

Filmmakers think if they just shoot one more project, get one more client, build one more reel, the money will follow. It does not work that way. The filmmakers charging $10K+ per project are not necessarily better than you. They just have a system for finding, pitching, and closing premium clients.
